Frequently Asked Questions

Your right- Our Responsibility

Which PA do I need?

That depends on how you intend to use it, how many people it will cater for and/ or the size of the venue. If you require the PA for background music or speeches and do not want it loud then you can go for the lower watts speakers. It is also worth noting that the wattage does not always determine the number of people it will be suitable for as sub woofers included in a system only increases the loudness of the low end or bass of the sound.

The PA i need is not listed?

All of our PA systems are made up of active speakers that are manufactured by WHARFEDALE and have matched components. This means that you can build up or down a PA system as you please or hire speakers individually to meet your individual requirements.

Do I need an amplifier?

No. Most of our speakers are active- this means that they have the amplifiers built in, so you will not require any additional or stand-alone amplifiers except for bass bins if required.

Can I power the PA system from a generator?

Yes, although you will need to check with your generator supplier that is suitable for powering sensitive electrical equipment, such as PA systems. This is different from a generator designed to power less sensitive electrical equipment such as power tools and bouncy castles.

Can I hire a generator from African Audio Works?

No, we do not currently provide generators. You can power our equipment from appropriate generators from own supplier.

Can I plug my IPOD or laptop into the PA?

Yes, you will need to hire a small mixer along with the speakers, which you can plug your IPOD, laptop Cd player etc. into before the signal goes to the PA.

Does someone come to set up the PA system?

If you book your order for delivery then we will set up and sound check the equipment for you free of charge.

Can I hire equipment for a house party?

Yes, you will need to book an appointment and arrive on time.

Do you give discounts?

Yes, we can provide discounts for not for profit and charity organizations and seasonal periods of more than 15% on all current rates.
